**Highlights**
In this study, a fungal species called Penicillium brevicompactum was grown in controlled fermentation systems. The research showed that when the growth medium was enriched with a specific amount of L-isoleucine, the fungus produced over 30% more MPA compared to the standard conditions. This improvement is important because MPA is used worldwide in medicine, especially for preventing organ-transplant rejection and treating viral and inflammatory conditions.
